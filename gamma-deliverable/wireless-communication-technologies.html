Wireless Communication Technologies

Wireless technologies are used in home automation products. Wireless technology transmits information between a smart lighting system and its control center.

There are many different wireless communication protocols. Not all products share the same protocol. Therefore, smart lighting products rely on a smart hub to connect a smart light system to its controlling device. In a smart lighting system, a controlling device is an app on a smart phone, a remote control, a smart wall switch, or a voice assistant.

Smart controls communicate with smart lighting through:

  • Voice assistants use a variety of protocols.
  • Smart phones use Wi-Fi or Bluetooth to connect to smart devices.
  • The example below shows how a smart hub bridges the communication between the voice assistant, smart phone, and smart lighting product that uses the Zigbee protocol.

Wireless Communication Protocols

Common wireless technology protocols used in smart lighting:

  • Matter
  • Zigbee
  • Z-Wave
  • Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE)
  • Wi-Fi
  • Thread
  • Lutron uses their own proprietary standard called ClearConnect.
