Smart Hubs

Smart hubs are the "control center" of smart home devices. They help reduce usage of Wi-Fi bandwidth from other devices and streamline smart home processes.

  • A smart hub connects smart devices to a voice assistant or app on your phone. The smart hub understands the languages of the communication protocols that smart devices use.

  • One app connects smart security, lights, window shades, fans, and door locks together through a smart hub.

  • Voice assistants and smart lights often operate on various protocols. A smart hub is necessary for products to connect to voice assistants.

  • The diagram below shows how a smart hub transfers information.

    • The smart hub collects the Matter protocol from the voice assistant and Wi-Fi protocol via a mobile app.
    • It translates both messages to the Zigbee protocol for the smart lights.
    • The remote control, smart switch, and smart lights communicate directly to each other through Zigbee.


Multi-protocol Hubs vs. Ecosystem Hubs

Multi-protocol Hubs

Multi-protocol Hubs connect many device platforms and centralize their control.

Examples of multi-protocol hubs:

  • Samsung SmartThings
  • HomeyPro
  • Hubitat
  • Home Assistant (open-source)

Multi-protocol hub example:

A home has 4 separate devices--a smart heating/cooling system, a smart garage door opener, Ring doorbell/camera, and Phillips Hue lighting system. A multi-protocol hub consolidates controls in a single app.

Ecosystem Hubs

Ecosystem Hubs are product brand dependent.

  • All of the devices must share the same product brand as the hub's brand.

Advantage: Devices controlled under an ecosystem hub have seamless integration and are user friendly.

Examples of ecosystem hubs and their product names:

Hub Ecosystem Voice Assistant
Amazon Echo Hub Amazon Echo Alexa
Google Nest Hub Google Nest Google Assistant
Apple Home Hub Apple HomeKit Siri
