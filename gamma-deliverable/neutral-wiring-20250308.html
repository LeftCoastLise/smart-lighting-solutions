Neutral Wiring

In-wall smart switches require installation of neutral wiring. For homes built before 1990, determine if the home has "neutral wiring" or "no neutral wiring." You can do this yourself or hire an electrician to verify for you.

  • Smart switches require a constant stream of voltage to power their "smart" components.
  • A neutral wire maintains active power to the smart switch.
  • Neutral wires exist to maintain voltage balance.
  • A neutral wire prevents overheating, damage to devices/appliances, and electrical fires.
  • To understand the differences between ground, neutral, and hot wires, watch Ground Neutral and Hot Wires Explained from The Engineering Mindset.

Safety Precautions:
  • Without a neutral wire to complete a circuit, the power outlet is at greater risk for delivering an electrical shock when you plug a power cord into the outlet.
  • Always turn off the circuit breaker before attempting any electrical work.
  • When in doubt, hire a professional electrician to assess, give advice, or help with installation.
