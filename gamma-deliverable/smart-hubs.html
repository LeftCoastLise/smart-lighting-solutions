Smart Hubs

  • A smart hub connects smart devices to a voice assistant or app on your phone. The smart hub understands the languages of the communication protocols that smart devices use.

  • A smart hub connects different smart home devices together. For example, your smart security, lights, window shades, fans, and door locks can all be controlled in one app when connected to a smart hub.

  • Smart hubs are necessary for smart lights to connect to voice assistants. Voice assistants and smart lights often operate on various protocols.

  • Smart hubs are the "control center" of smart home devices. They help reduce usage of Wi-Fi bandwidth from other devices and streamline smart home processes.

  • The diagram below shows how a smart hub transfers information. The smart hub collects the Matter protocol from the voice assistant and Wi-Fi protocol from the phone. Then it translates both messages to the Zigbee protocol for the smart lights. The remote control, smart switch, and smart lights are all hardware that can communicate directly to each other through Zigbee.


Multi-protocol Hubs vs. Ecosystem Hubs

Multi-protocol Hubs connect many device platforms and centralize their control.

  • Example: If your home has 4 separate devices--a smart heating/cooling system, a smart garage door opener, Ring doorbell/camera, and Phillips Hue lighting system--you can use a multi-protocol hub to consolidate controls in a single app.
  • Multi-protocol Hub products:
    • Samsung SmartThings
    • HomeyPro
    • Hubitat
    • Home Assistant (open-source)

    Ecosystem Hubs are product brand dependent. All of the devices must share the same product brand as the hub's brand. Advantage: Devices controlled under an ecosystem hub have seamless integration and are user friendly.
